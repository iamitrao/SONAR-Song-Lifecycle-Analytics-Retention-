# Executive Summary: Spain Top 50 Songs Data Project
**Prepared for:** Government Stakeholders & Atlantic Recording Corporation
**By:** Amit Kumar

## Project Overview

For my internship project, I looked closely at how songs go up and down the Spotify Spain Top 50 charts. I wanted to see if the Spanish music market works differently. Based on the data, the Spanish charts change really fast, and how long a song stays popular depends a lot on whether it’s a single or if it has swearing in it. 

## What the Data Showed Me

1. **Songs Enter and Leave Quickly**
Every day, about **2.1%** of the playlist gets replaced by new songs. This might seem small, but it adds up quickly! I calculated that only **36%** of songs can stay on the playlist for more than 30 days. This means the Spanish charts love fresh hits, and it's hard for older songs to stay around forever.

2. **Songs Peak Extremely Fast**
When a song does get popular, it happens almost immediately. My analysis shows that it takes only **9.5 days** on average for a song to hit its highest spot. If a song doesn't get popular in its first week and a half, it probably isn't going to be a huge hit. 

## My Thoughts on Strategy

- **Clean vs. Explicit Songs:** Even though a lot of popular Latin songs are explicit, my data shows that "**Clean**" songs actually stay on the playlist longer! Explicit songs survive about 10% less time than clean ones. So, making clean versions of songs might be a smart move for keeping songs on the chart longer.
- **Singles Are Best:** Releasing singles works way better than releasing a whole album at once. I found that singles stay on the Top 50 an amazing **1.5 times longer** than individual album tracks do. It seems listeners in Spain prefer focusing on one great track at a time!

## Conclusion

To sum it all up: Atlantic Records should focus all their marketing energy on the first 10 days of a song's release. Also, instead of dropping huge albums, giving the Spanish market high-quality single tracks (and keeping them clean!) is the best way to fight against how fast songs drop off the charts.
